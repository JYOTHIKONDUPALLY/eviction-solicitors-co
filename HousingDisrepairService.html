Housing Disrepair Counterclaims in Eviction Proceedings

England & Wales – 2025 Guide

When landlords begin possession proceedings, tenants often raise a counterclaim for housing disrepair. This legal defence can significantly affect the outcome of the case, sometimes reducing rent arrears, delaying eviction, or even dismissing proceedings entirely.

Get Expert Legal Advice

Both landlords and tenants must understand the interplay between eviction claims and disrepair counterclaims. The court will assess compliance with repair obligations, landlord duties, and tenant rights under the Housing Act 1988, the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985, and subsequent case law.

Landlord Duties and Risks in Disrepair Counterclaims

Landlords have statutory and contractual obligations to keep rental properties in good repair. In eviction proceedings, failure to meet these duties can expose landlords to:

  • Set-off claims – where rent arrears are reduced by compensation for disrepair.
  • Damages awards – for inconvenience, health impacts, or loss of amenity.
  • Adjournment or dismissal of possession proceedings – where disrepair is serious and unresolved.
  • Additional costs – including expert surveyor fees and legal expenses.
High Risk Warning

Landlords who ignore repair obligations may face significant financial penalties and legal consequences in eviction proceedings.

Tenant Rights in Raising a Counterclaim

Tenants facing eviction may:

  • Allege disrepair as a defence to rent arrears, arguing that the landlord breached repair obligations.
  • Request inspection and expert evidence to support their case.
  • Seek compensation for damp, mould, leaks, structural defects, or health issues caused by poor housing conditions.
  • Delay possession proceedings until the disrepair claim is heard and determined.
  • Apply to set off damages against arrears, reducing the landlord's entitlement.

Tenant Obligations in Disrepair Cases

Although tenants may raise disrepair counterclaims, they also carry responsibilities:

  • Reporting defects promptly to the landlord or agent.
  • Allowing reasonable access for repairs to be carried out.
  • Maintaining the property in a tenant-like manner.
  • Providing evidence (e.g. photographs, medical records, expert reports) when asserting a counterclaim.

How the Court Handles Disrepair Counterclaims in 2025

The modern eviction process often intersects with disrepair claims as follows:

1
Possession Claim Issued

Usually under Section 8, Ground 8 (rent arrears), initiating formal eviction proceedings.

2
Tenant Files Defence and Counterclaim

Alleging disrepair and requesting damages to offset against rent arrears.

3
Court Directions

The judge may order disclosure of repair records, expert reports, or property inspections.

4
Hearing of Both Claims Together

Possession and disrepair are often dealt with in the same proceedings for efficiency.

5
Court Outcome

Conclusion

In eviction proceedings, housing disrepair counterclaims can drastically change outcomes. Landlords risk delays, cost awards, and reduced arrears if repair duties have not been met, while tenants may gain compensation or extended time in the property.

Both parties should seek early legal advice, supported by expert surveyors and solicitors, to protect their position.
