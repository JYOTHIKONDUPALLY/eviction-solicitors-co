What is Rent Arrears Recovery?

Recovering possession and unpaid rent often starts with standard notices, but many landlords face complex cases involving informal agreements, lodgers, squatters, HMOs, and disputed arrears.

Why Some Cases Fall Outside Standard Eviction Processes

We provide a full range of services for complicated eviction cases, including:

Informal Agreements

No formal contracts or tenancy documentation requiring bespoke legal strategies.

Complex Occupations

Lodgers, licence holders, or implied tenants with varied legal statuses.

HMO Situations

Houses in Multiple Occupation with various licence or tenancy arrangements.

Squatters & Trespassers

Unauthorized occupants with no legal tenancy requiring specialized removal procedures.

Disputed Arrears

Multi-party liability scenarios and missing tenant complications.

Tenant Tracing

Locating absconded tenants and pursuing enforcement across jurisdictions.

Enforcement Options After Possession Order

Possession Enforcement

  • County Court Bailiffs: Standard enforcement with 4-12 weeks wait time
  • High Court Enforcement Officers: Faster execution within 7 days (requires Form N293A transfer)
  • Urgent Cases: Ideal for high-value arrears requiring immediate action

Money Judgment Enforcement

  • County Court Judgment (CCJ): Affects credit score for 6 years, motivates payment
  • Writ/Warrant of Control: Seize goods or arrange controlled goods agreements
  • Attachment of Earnings: Direct deductions from debtor's wages
  • Third-Party Debt Order: Freeze funds in debtor's bank accounts
Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Failing to promptly register CCJ, using wrong enforcement route, attempting collection from unsupported legal obligations, not using tracing services when tenant absconds, or ignoring HMO licensing requirements.
